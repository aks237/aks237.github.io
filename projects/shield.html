--- layout: project title: "Shield the Bay" ---

Shield the Bay was an initiative launched during the early days of the COVID-19 pandemic to address the critical shortages of personal protective equipment (PPE) faced by healthcare workers. Led by Berkeley Professor Jaspal Sandhu, the project focused on the production and distribution of 3D-printed face shield visors. These visors were designed to hold clear plastic sheets, forming effective and affordable face guards for frontline workers.

I contributed to the initiative by actively 3D printing face shield visors and onboarding other individuals with 3D printers to scale up production. Collaborating with a motivated team, we provided essential PPE to healthcare facilities, alleviating some of the strain caused by the global supply chain disruptions.

Impact and Acknowledgments

Shield the Bay was an inspiring example of community-driven innovation during a time of global crisis. By combining modern technology, such as 3D printing, with the collaborative spirit of volunteers, we were able to support healthcare workers when they needed it most.

I am grateful to Professor Jaspal Sandhu for his leadership and to the many contributors who joined forces to make this initiative a success.
