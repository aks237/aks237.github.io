--- layout: project title: "Audio Interference Car" ---

The Audio Interference Car was created for an experimental performance by Cornell's Department of Music. This robot was designed to create physical and acoustic interference between a speaker emitting different frequencies and a microphone, producing unique sounds as part of the performance. Its movement was randomized, and it skillfully avoided people in its path using ultrasonic sensors.

The car's structure included a drive base with two motors, wheels, and a caster ball for stability. It supported a metal frame with a foam sheet to act as a physical interference medium. This design allowed for dynamic, real-time interaction between the speaker and microphone, adding an experimental layer to the performance's sonic experience.
